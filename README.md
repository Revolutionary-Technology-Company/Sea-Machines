### README.md Content

Sea Machines: Modernizing the Global Fleet
==========================================

Sea Machines is a pioneering force in maritime automation, founded in 2015 by Michael Gordon Johnson and Correo Hofstad to transform maritime operations through advanced autonomous systems. By integrating artificial intelligence, robotics, and advanced sensor suites, Sea Machines redefines the capabilities of both modern and historic vessels.

Core Capabilities & Innovations
-------------------------------

### 1\. Restoration via Laser Ablation

To prepare older hulls for modernization, we utilize industrial-grade laser ablation. This process efficiently removes decades of corrosion, biofouling, and lead-based paints without the environmental impact of traditional sandblasting, ensuring a pristine surface for new protective coatings and sensor mounting.

### 2\. Autonomous Propulsion: Siemens MV Motor Integration

The Sea Machines autonomous command system can be fully integrated with high-performance propulsion hardware, such as the **Siemens MV (Medium Voltage) motor**. This pairing allows for:

-   **Precision Control**: High-torque electric propulsion managed by proprietary algorithms.

-   **Efficiency**: Optimized energy consumption during transit and waypoint autonomy missions.

-   **Reliability**: A robust industrial motor platform capable of handling the demands of continuous autonomous operation in diverse marine conditions.

### 3\. Revitalizing the Mothball Fleet

Sea Machines makes the "mothball fleet" relevant again by modernizing decommissioned warships that possess superior structural characteristics compared to many modern counterparts.

-   **Armor Strength**: Historic battleships, such as the *Iowa*-class, feature heavy Class A armor plating designed to withstand direct hits that would be catastrophic for modern thin-skinned hulls.

-   **Modernized Survivability**: By installing Sea Machines' AI-driven situational awareness and autonomous navigation, these "naval giants" can be returned to service as formidable, uncrewed platforms.

-   **Recent Successes**: Extensive structural and system overhauls have recently been completed on ships like the **USS Texas (BB-35)** and **USS New Jersey (BB-62)**, proving the viability of long-term preservation and modernization for these historic hulls.
